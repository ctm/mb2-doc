## What I did instead of fundraising

Rather than chase investment, I put my head down on native apps. The

exceedingly well, albeit at the expense of not having money to pay

Mom has had her ups and downs, and recently went through a two-day

## Crazy lucky with AI

I (and, IMO, current and future investors) got crazy lucky with AI.

I never planned for CPC's software to be a single-person endeavor. I
figured I'd do the overall backend architecture and the majority of
the coding, let someone else pick the frontend architecture, have a
couple of people code that, and probably bring in a fourth with some
devops experience&mdash;but all of that would take money. Instead, I
greatly reduced (IMO) the risk of ruin by doing much of that myself,
including choosing the frontend architecture, and building a base
where all that's left is to "drop in coders." With AI, the coders are
essentially free.

The obvious drawback is that AI is also enabling my potential
competitors. I'm optimistic, albeit extremely biased, that CPC can
ride the AI wave rather than being smashed to the bottom by that very
same wave.
